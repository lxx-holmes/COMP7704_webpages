McDonald's (MCD) Stock Analysis

Stock Price: $290-$300 March 2024

Executive Summary and Recommendation

Investment Recommendation: HOLD (Neutral)

Current fundamentals remain exceptional, but valuation is at the high end of historical ranges.

McDonald's has demonstrated strong financial performance over the past five years, recovering swiftly from the 2020 downturn to reach record sales and profits. Revenue has grown at a ~5% CAGR (2020-2024), driven by robust comparable sales and steady unit expansion.

Operating margins returned to the mid-40% range by 2023, with net income reaching ~$8.5B. The franchise model keeps costs low and margins high, even amid inflation.

Recent Financial Performance

Revenue (2024) $25.92B
Net Income (2023) $8.47B
Operating Margin ~46%

McDonald's has shown strong post-pandemic revenue recovery, with sales rebounding from $19.2 billion in 2020 to $25.92 billion in 2024. After a 21% surge in 2021, growth stalled in 2022 due to Russia's exit but picked up again in 2023 (+9.97%).

Balance Sheet

McDonald's maintains strong cash flow, supported by its high-margin franchise model. Operating cash flow rebounded from $6.3B in 2020 to $9.61B in 2023, while free cash flow (FCF) reached $7.25B in 2023.

Key Balance Sheet Metrics

  • Long-term debt: $38.5B (2024)
  • Interest coverage ratio: 7.7× (2024)
  • Debt-to-EBITDA ratio: ~2.75× (2024)

Stock Performance

McDonald's stock has delivered strong returns with low volatility over the past five years. After dropping to $124 during the March 2020 crash, it rebounded to $197 by year-end 2020 (+11%). The rally continued, with MCD closing 2021 at ~$252 (+28%) and reaching ~$291 in 2023 (+15%).

"Long-term investors should hold for steady returns (~9% annual total return potential, including dividends), while new investors may wait for a market pullback."
