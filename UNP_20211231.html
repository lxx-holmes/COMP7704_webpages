Key Items

- No significant changes (greater than ¬±5%) in percentage of total revenue or assets/liabilities composition between 2020-2021.
- Total revenue increased 11.62% ($19,533M to $21,804M) with operating income up 19.21% ($7,834M to $9,338M) and net income rising 21.94% ($5,349M to $6,523M).
- Total shareholders' equity decreased 16.48% ($16,958M to $14,161M) year-over-year.
- Double-digit growth across all key income metrics occurred without major shifts in revenue/expense proportions.
- Shareholders' equity reduction contrasts with strong operational performance improvements.

Profitability Analysis

- Net profit margin increased from 27.4% (2020) to 30.0% (2021), driven by 11.6% revenue growth and effective expense management.
- Operating profit margin rose from 39.9% to 42.8%, reflecting controlled operating expenses, particularly reduced fuel and compensation costs.
- Return on Assets (ROA) improved from 8.6% to 10.3%, indicating enhanced asset utilization through operational excellence initiatives.
- Return on Equity (ROE) surged from 31.6% to 46.1%, partly due to reduced total equity (from $16,958M to $14,161M) alongside profit growth.
- Revenue expansion and cost containment strategies contributed to margin improvements across all profitability metrics.
- Equity reduction amplified ROE performance despite being a balance sheet adjustment rather than operational improvement.
- Operational efficiency gains and disciplined expense management supported simultaneous growth in profits and margins.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current ratio dropped from 1.010 (2020) to 0.619 (2021), quick ratio from 0.856 to 0.510, and cash ratio from 0.431 to 0.167 due to 47% cash reserve decline and 37% current liabilities increase.
- Cash and equivalents fell from $1,799M (2020) to $960M (2021), reducing cash coverage of short-term obligations.
- Working capital shifted from surplus (2020 COVID precautionary cash) to deficit (2021) driven by upcoming debt maturities.
- $7.3B share repurchases and $2.8B dividends drained cash reserves despite $9.0B operating cash flow generation.
- Debt exchange ($1.7B) and long-term issuance ($3.5B) increased overall debt burden while short-term liquidity remained pressured.
- Reduced COVID-19 uncertainty in 2021 led to lower cash holdings compared to 2020's elevated pandemic reserves.
- Management relied on credit facilities to meet obligations, highlighting liquidity strain from imbalanced cash deployment.
- Current ratio below 1 (0.619) in 2021 reflects insufficient current assets to cover $3.4B current liabilities.
- Operational cash flow prioritized for shareholder returns rather than liquidity maintenance, exacerbating cash position decline.
- Combined 37% current liabilities growth and 47% cash reduction strained ratios, requiring urgent working capital management focus.

Solvency Analysis

- Debt-to-Equity ratio increased from 2.68 (2020) to 3.48 (2021) due to $3.5B long-term debt issuance, $1.7B debt exchange, and $7.3B share repurchases reducing equity.
- Total liabilities rose from $45,440M (2020) to $49,364M (2021), while equity fell from $16,958M to $14,161M.
- Interest Coverage Ratio improved from 8.81x (2020) to 9.97x (2021) as EBIT increased (+$1,504M) while interest expense remained stable ($1,141M to $1,157M).
- Share repurchases of $7.3B in 2021 outweighed $6,523M net income, driving equity reduction despite profitability.
- Solvency ratio (liabilities/equity) worsened from 2.68 (2020) to 3.48 (2021), reflecting increased reliance on debt financing.
- Liquidity remains strong with operating cash flow and credit facilities, but higher leverage elevates financial risk.
- Debt strategy aimed to refinance and lower interest costs, while equity reduction prioritized shareholder returns via buybacks and dividends.
- Mixed solvency trends: improved interest coverage contrasts with higher leverage, requiring monitoring of long-term stability.

Efficiency Analysis

- DSO increased from 28.1 days (2020) to 28.9 days (2021), indicating a slight slowdown in accounts receivable collection but minimal cash flow impact.
- DIO dropped from 71.0 days (2020) to 55.8 days (2021), reflecting faster inventory turnover and improved inventory management, freeing cash.
- DPO decreased from 345.6 days (2020) to 321.4 days (2021), suggesting faster supplier payments that may strain cash flow but signal tighter cash management.
- CCC improved from -246.5 days (2020) to -236.7 days (2021), driven by inventory efficiency and payables control, maintaining reliance on supplier financing.
- Cash/cash equivalents fell from $1,799M (2020) to $960M (2021) despite higher net income and operating cash flow, indicating robust profitability and active cash allocation.
- Financial statements cite enhanced internal controls and management practices as contributors to improved working capital efficiency.
- Negative CCC underscores effective use of supplier credit to fund operations, with reduced cycle intensity reflecting balanced cash flow optimization.
