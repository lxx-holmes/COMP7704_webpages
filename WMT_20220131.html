Solvency Analysis

- Debt-to-Equity ratio decreased from 1.88 (2021) to 1.67 (2022) due to $6.6 billion long-term debt reduction, including early extinguishment of high-interest debt, and equity increases from share buybacks and $3.2 billion Flipkart stock sale.

- Solvency ratio improved from 1.53 to 1.60 as cash reserves grew to $14.8 billion (2022) and total liabilities decreased through strategic debt repayment and working capital optimization.
- $6.945 billion in new low-interest long-term debt was issued while $13.01 billion of existing debt was repaid/retired, reducing refinancing risk and interest burden.
- Short-term borrowings plummeted from $4.048 billion (2021) to $0.716 billion (2022), enhancing liquidity and reducing near-term repayment obligations.
- Proactive capital structure management included selling non-core assets (Flipkart shares) and prioritizing early retirement of high-cost debt to optimize financial flexibility.

- Mixed efficiency signals: While CCC improvement reflects stronger cash management, slower receivables collection and inventory turnover require operational review to sustain long-term efficiency.
