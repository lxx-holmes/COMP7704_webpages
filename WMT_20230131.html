- Total current liabilities increased from 35.7% to 38.0% of total liabilities/equity (+2.3 percentage points), while Walmart shareholders' equity declined from 34.0% to 31.5% (-2.5 percentage points).
- Total assets decreased 0.7% year-over-year (not significant), with liabilities increasing 2.8% and shareholders' equity decreasing 8.85% (below 10% significance threshold).
- No material changes (>¬±5%) in revenue composition percentages between 2022 and 2023 were identified.

Profitability Analysis

- Net Profit Margin decreased from 2.39% (2022) to 1.91% (2023), driven by divestiture-related losses (Asda/Seiyu sales) and external economic pressures, reducing income conversion efficiency.
- Gross Profit Margin declined from 25.09% to 24.13%, reflecting inflation-driven cost increases and supply chain challenges impacting cost of goods sold.
- Operating Profit Margin dropped sharply from 4.53% to 3.34% due to rising operating expenses from e-commerce investments, omni-channel initiatives, and potential labor cost increases.
- Return on Assets (ROA) fell from 5.58% to 4.80%, indicating less efficient asset utilization linked to divestiture-related asset write-downs and reduced net income.
- Return on Equity (ROE) slightly decreased from 14.87% to 13.94% despite stock buybacks, as net income declines from divestitures and cost pressures outweighed equity reduction benefits.
- All profitability ratios declined, signaling operational inefficiencies from higher costs, strategic investments, and $2.7B in divestiture pre-tax losses per Note 12 disclosures.
- External factors including inflation, recessionary risks, and shifting consumer spending patterns exacerbated margin compression across revenue and cost structures.
- Asset utilization challenges emerged from reduced operational scale post-divestitures, impacting returns despite revenue growth in core segments.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current Ratio declined from 0.93 (2022) to 0.82 (2023), driven by a $4.8B increase in current liabilities (reaching $92,198M) and reduced cash reserves.
- Quick Ratio fell from 0.28 (2022) to 0.21 (2023) due to a $6.1B drop in cash/cash equivalents and less liquid current asset composition.
- Cash Ratio dropped sharply from 0.169 (2022) to 0.093 (2023) as cash reserves decreased 41.6% ($14,760M to $8,625M), partly from $17.7B in 2023 investments/acquisitions versus $6.0B in 2022.
- $3.3B accrued liability for opioid-related lawsuits contributed to rising current liabilities, exacerbating liquidity pressures.
- 62% of 2023 cash held outside the U.S. (some non-transferable), reducing readily available liquidity.
- Shift in current assets toward less liquid components (e.g., inventory, receivables) weakened Quick Ratio without inventory reliance.
- Operational cash flow challenges emerged from increased capital expenditures and reduced cash flexibility for short-term obligations.

Solvency Analysis

- Walmart's debt-to-equity ratio increased from 1.66 (2022) to 1.90 (2023) due to a $4.969 billion long-term debt issuance in September 2022, increasing total liabilities to $159,206M and reducing equity to $83,754M.
- Interest coverage ratio declined from 14.13 to 10.91 times as EBIT fell from $25,942M to $20,428M (2023) despite stable interest expenses ($1,874M vs. $1,836M), reflecting lower profitability.
- Debt-to-asset ratio rose from 62% to 65% as liabilities grew to $159,206M against assets of $243,197M, driven by net debt increases from new borrowings and repayments.
- Long-term debt increased by $1.2 billion in 2023, contributing to higher financial leverage and reduced solvency buffer, though interest coverage remains strong.
- Asset base slightly decreased from $244,860M (2022) to $243,197M (2023), amplifying the impact of liability growth on debt-to-asset metrics.
- While Walmart maintains operational capacity to service debt, increased reliance on debt financing and lower EBIT warrant closer monitoring of leverage trends.

Efficiency Analysis

- DSO decreased from 13.8 to 12.4 days in 2023, reflecting improved collections due to stricter credit policies or early payment incentives.
- DIO declined from 47.8 to 44.6 days, indicating faster inventory turnover driven by supply chain improvements and streamlined logistics.
- DPO reduced from 46.3 to 43.8 days, suggesting better supplier negotiations or optimized payment processing despite shorter payment terms.
- Cash Conversion Cycle improved from 15.3 to 13.2 days, demonstrating enhanced working capital efficiency through combined receivables, inventory, and payables management.
- Operational drivers included supply chain automation investments and divestitures of international operations, allowing focus on higher-efficiency markets.
