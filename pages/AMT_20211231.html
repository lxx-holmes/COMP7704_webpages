Profitability Analysis

- Net profit margin increased from 21.02% (2020) to 27.47% (2021) due to improved revenue recognition (straight-line lease accounting) and effective cost management despite revenue growth
- ROA improved slightly from 3.58% to 3.67% through more efficient asset utilization supported by rigorous valuation methods (replacement costs, discounted cash flows) and asset lifecycle management
- ROE decreased from 37.04% to 28.3% as equity base expanded faster than net income growth, driven by investments in data center assets and retained earnings
- Gross profit margin remained strong despite slight decline (72.3% to 71.35%), reflecting minor COGS increases offset by maintained pricing power and cost control
- Operating profit margin fell from 35.88% to 33.46% due to operating expense growth outpacing revenue, linked to capital expenditures and infrastructure expansion costs
- High gross margin (71.35% in 2021) underscores sustained pricing strategy effectiveness and operational cost discipline across periods
- Revenue recognition policies (particularly straight-line lease revenue accounting) directly contributed to improved net earnings and margin expansion
- Increased equity base from property investments and retained earnings diluted ROE despite improved absolute profitability metrics

- Short-term liquidity weakened significantly, indicating challenges in meeting obligations and requiring improved cash reserves, liability management, and operational cash flow.
