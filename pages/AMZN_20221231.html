üìä Financials

Key Items

- Other Income (Expense), Net decreased 6.38% of total revenue (3.11% in 2021 to -3.27% in 2022), contributing to a 7.63% decline in Net Income margin (7.10% to -0.53%).
- Gross Profit grew 14.52% YoY ($197,478 to $225,152), but Income from Operations fell 50.73% ($24,879 to $12,248) and Net Income plummeted 91.83% ($33,364 to -$2,722).
- Marketable securities declined sharply from 14.2% to 3.5% of total assets (-10.7% shift), while total current assets decreased from 38.4% to 31.7% (-6.7% shift).
- Total Assets grew 10.02% YoY ($420,549 to $462,675), outpaced by Total Liabilities growth of 12.17% ($282,304 to $316,632).
- Shareholders' Equity increased 5.64% YoY ($138,245 to $146,043), reflecting slower growth compared to liabilities.

Profitability Analysis

- Net profit margin declined from 7.1% (2021) to -0.5% (2022) due to increased operating expenses, $1.1B impairments in physical stores, and contract termination/severance costs.
- Operating profit margin decreased from 5.3% to 2.4%, driven by rising fulfillment costs (higher wages, infrastructure), technology investments, and marketing expenses.
- Return on Assets (ROA) fell from 8.0% to -0.6%, reflecting inefficient asset utilization and impairments impacting profitability despite higher sales.
- Return on Equity (ROE) dropped from 24.1% to -1.9% as net income turned negative, significantly reducing shareholder returns.
- Gross profit margin improved slightly from 42.0% to 43.8%, but higher cost of sales ($288.8B in 2022 vs. $272.3B in 2021) limited overall profitability gains.
- Key drivers included $1.1B in property/equipment impairments, increased fulfillment and tech costs (payroll, infrastructure), and higher cost of sales outpacing revenue growth.
- Foreign exchange fluctuations contributed marginally to expense increases but were secondary to operational cost pressures.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current ratio declined from 1.14 (2021) to 0.94 (2022) due to reduced current assets ($161.6B to $146.8B) and higher current liabilities ($142.3B to $155.4B), driven by lower marketable securities and increased payables.
- Quick ratio fell from 0.91 to 0.72 as marketable securities plummeted $59.8B to $16.1B, outweighing cash/cash equivalents growth, reflecting weaker liquidity excluding inventories.
- Cash ratio improved from 0.25 to 0.35 due to higher cash reserves, but overall liquidity remained constrained by rising liabilities and asset declines.
- Net income dropped from $33.4B profit to $2.7B loss in 2022, impacted by $13.4B impairment losses and $2.1B severance costs, reducing operational cash flow.
- Current liabilities increased 9.2% to $155.4B, primarily from higher accounts payable and accrued expenses, intensifying short-term obligations.
- Operating cash flow weakened despite remaining positive, with significant $61.7B invested in property/equipment and debt activity shifts, prioritizing long-term growth over short-term liquidity.
- Marketable securities reduction ($59.8B to $16.1B) was the largest contributor to declining current assets, partially offset by cash/cash equivalents growth.

Solvency Analysis

- Debt-to-Equity ratio increased from 2.04x (2021) to 2.17x (2022) due to $12.8B and $8.3B debt issuances in April/December 2022, raising long-term debt from $48.7B to $67.2B, while equity only grew modestly from $138.2B to $146.0B.
- Interest Coverage Ratio fell sharply from 13.75x to 5.17x as EBIT dropped 51% ($24.9B to $12.2B) and interest expense rose 31% ($1.8B to $2.4B), driven by higher operating costs and $2.6B impairment/severance expenses.
- Solvency Ratio worsened from 2.04x to 2.17x with liabilities growing faster (+12%) than equity (+6%), as total liabilities increased from $282.3B to $316.6B.
- Increased leverage reflects $63.6B in 2022 property/equipment investments and $8.3B acquisitions, funded partly by debt despite $36.8B operating cash flow.
- Profitability decline contributed to reduced coverage capacity, with operating income falling 50% year-over-year due to inflationary pressures and operational restructuring costs.
- Higher financial risk emerges from debt dependency, with total debt representing 46% of 2022 liabilities ($67.2B/$316.6B), up from 40% in 2021.

Efficiency Analysis

- DSO increased from 26 days (2021) to 30 days (2022), indicating slower receivables collection; sales growth outpaced collections despite $21.9B receivables decrease in 2022 vs. $18.2B in 2021.
- DIO remained stable at 43 days both years, reflecting consistent inventory turnover; inventory decreases slowed to $2.6B in 2022 vs. $9.5B in 2021.
- DPO decreased from 104 days (2021) to 99 days (2022), suggesting faster supplier payments; accounts payable growth slowed to $2.9B in 2022 vs. $3.6B in 2021.
- CCC deteriorated from -35 days (2021) to -26 days (2022) due to higher DSO and lower DPO, despite stable operating cash flows (~$46.7B in 2022 vs. $46.3B in 2021).
- Increased short-term debt borrowings in 2022 provided liquidity but were partially offset by tighter supplier payment terms.
- Stable inventory management aligned with efficient supply chain operations despite CCC efficiency decline.
- Negative CCC in both years maintained overall cash management strength, though 2022 showed reduced optimization.
