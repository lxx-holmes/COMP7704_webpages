- Strategic investments in marketing and customer rewards programs drove higher spending volumes and strong customer retention rates.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current Ratio remained unchanged at 0.778 in 2020 and 2021 due to stable current assets and liabilities, though below 1 suggests potential liquidity challenges.
- Quick Ratio stayed at 0.778 both years, reflecting consistent coverage of short-term obligations with liquid assets (excluding inventory) matching liability stability.
- Cash Ratio declined from 0.335 (2020) to 0.226 (2021) as cash/cash equivalents dropped 33% ($32,965M to $22,028M), reducing immediate cash liquidity.
- Stable current assets/liabilities maintained Current and Quick Ratios, while the $10.9B cash reserve reduction drove the Cash Ratio decline.
- Overall liquidity remains stable with manageable short-term obligations, but the cash position weakening requires monitoring despite broader liquidity consistency.

- Increased leverage and reduced equity reflect strategic capital management focused on shareholder returns, balanced by stronger profitability and interest coverage capacity.
