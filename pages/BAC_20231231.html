Liquidity Analysis

- Interest Coverage Ratio plummeted from 1.54 (2022) to 0.386 (2023), falling below 1 due to higher effective interest rates on debt and potential EBIT declines, indicating EBIT insufficient to cover interest expenses.
- Equity Ratio improved slightly from 8.95% (2022) to 9.17% (2023), driven by increased retained earnings and preferred stock issuance partially offsetting share repurchases, enhancing equity financing relative to assets.
- Despite controlled debt growth and equity strengthening, the drastic Interest Coverage Ratio decline signals acute solvency risk, requiring urgent monitoring of interest expense trends and operating income performance.
- Rising interest expenses (linked to higher debt rates) and potential EBIT contraction are critical stressors, overshadowing marginal improvements in capital structure metrics like Debt-to-Equity and Equity Ratios.
