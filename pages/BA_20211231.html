Profitability Analysis

- Net Profit Margin improved from -20.88% (2020) to -7.15% (2021), reflecting reduced net losses from $11,873M to $4,202M due to better cost management and operational efficiency.
- Operating Profit Margin rose from -21.94% (2020) to -4.66% (2021), with operating losses decreasing from $12,767M to $2,902M, signaling improved expense control.
- Gross Profit Margin turned positive at 4.90% (2021) from -9.69% (2020), indicating better direct cost management and revenue coverage of production costs.
- Return on Assets (ROA) improved from -7.80% (2020) to -3.04% (2021), showing marginally better asset utilization despite ongoing unprofitability.
- Negative Return on Equity (ROE) persisted due to shareholder equity deficits, reflecting financial distress.
- COVID-19 pandemic severely impacted 2020 operations, reducing air travel demand and revenues, while 2021 saw partial recovery through efficiency gains.
- Despite 2021 improvements, all profitability metrics remained negative, highlighting persistent financial strain and the need for strategic turnaround efforts.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current ratio declined from 1.40 (2020) to 1.33 (2021) due to a 10.7% drop in current assets ($121,642M to $108,666M) from reduced commercial deliveries and 787/777X program delays, outpacing current liabilities reduction ($87,280M to $81,992M).
- Quick ratio fell sharply from 0.46 to 0.36 as inventory buildup (undelivered 787/777X aircraft) reduced liquid assets, leaving only $108,666M - inventories to cover $81,992M liabilities.
- Cash ratio marginally improved from 0.088 to 0.098 with cash reserves rising from $7,752M to $8,052M, though still insufficient relative to $81,992M liabilities despite $9.8B debt issuance for refinancing.
- Operational challenges included $3.4B negative operating cash flow (2021), pandemic-driven air travel declines, and 737 MAX grounding impacts, limiting liquidity recovery.
- Inventory management pressures grew with $8,052M cash representing only 9.8% of current liabilities, forcing reliance on non-cash assets and slowing production rates to conserve liquidity.
- Strategic cash conservation measures included workforce reductions, suspended share buybacks, and debt management, but failed to offset $58.1B total debt burden and program-specific delays.
- Liquidity erosion highlighted need for improved aircraft delivery rates, inventory monetization, or liability reductions to strengthen the 1.33 current ratio and 0.36 quick ratio positioning.

Solvency Analysis

- Debt-to-Equity ratio worsened from -9.42x (2020) to -10.33x (2021) due to liabilities decreasing $16.8B but equity deficit remaining severe ($14.8B vs $18.1B), reflecting persistent negative equity and insolvency risk.
- Interest Coverage Ratio improved from -7.67x to -2.87x (2021) driven by reduced net loss ($4.3B vs $11.9B) and $13.8B debt repayment, though EBIT (-$7.7B) still failed to cover interest expenses.
- Financial Leverage Ratio deteriorated from -8.42x to -9.32x (2021) as total assets declined $13.6B while equity deficit narrowed insufficiently, amplifying leverage risks despite liquidity management efforts.
- Total liabilities decreased $16.8B in 2021 through $9.8B senior note issuance and $13.8B term loan repayment, improving liquidity ($16.2B cash + $14.7B credit capacity) without resolving structural solvency issues.

- Times Interest Earned Ratio remained negative at -2.87x (2021) vs -7.67x (2020), showing persistent failure to cover interest obligations through operating profits despite reduced interest expenses.

Efficiency Analysis

- Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) increased from -14.9 days (2020) to 7.0 days (2021), driven by reduced Days Payable Outstanding (DPO), slower collections, and inventory turnover amid operational disruptions.
- DPO decreased from 74.0 days (2020) to 57.1 days (2021), reflecting faster supplier payments, which shortened CCC but were offset by slower receivables and inventory turnover.
- Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) rose from 12.3 days (2020) to 15.5 days (2021), indicating prolonged customer collections and contributing to longer CCC.
- Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO) increased from 46.8 days (2020) to 48.6 days (2021) due to 737 MAX/787 program delays, production disruptions, and inventory buildup, extending CCC.
- COVID-19 reduced air travel demand, leading to lower aircraft deliveries, negative operating cash flows, and production rate cuts, impairing working capital efficiency.
- Production challenges included $1.4 billion in 737 MAX reach-forward losses and 787 delivery pauses, exacerbating inventory turnover delays and liquidity strain.
- Boeing issued debt to bolster liquidity but faced high leverage and negative cash flows, complicating cash management despite improved payables efficiency.
- Payables management improvements partially offset slower receivables and inventory turnover, but overall CCC increase highlights persistent operational and market headwinds.
