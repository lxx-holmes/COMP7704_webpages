Liquidity Analysis

- Current Ratio declined from 1.22x (2022) to 1.14x (2023), driven by increased current liabilities (accrued liabilities rose from $21,581M to $22,331M) and stable current assets despite revenue growth.
- Quick Ratio dropped from 0.35x (2022) to 0.31x (2023), reflecting reduced cash/cash equivalents ($14,614M to $12,691M) and exclusion of higher inventory balances from liquid assets.
- Higher inventories tied up cash, reducing liquidity buffers and disproportionately impacting the Quick Ratio.
- Increased customer concessions and considerations related to the 737 MAX program contributed to rising current liabilities.
- Stable current assets failed to offset liability growth, compressing liquidity ratios.
- Operating Cash Flow Ratio could not be calculated due to insufficient operating cash flow data disclosure.
- Declining liquidity signals reduced financial flexibility, challenging Boeing‚Äôs ability to invest or manage short-term obligations.
- Working capital management, particularly inventory optimization and liability control, is critical for liquidity improvement.

Solvency Analysis

- Debt-to-Equity ratio was not meaningful in 2023 and 2022 due to negative equity, with liabilities exceeding assets both years, signaling severe financial instability.
- Debt-to-Asset ratio increased from 1.115 (2022) to 1.125 (2023), driven by a $5.2B rise in long-term debt to $52,799M, reflecting higher financial leverage.
- Interest Coverage Ratio remained negative in both years due to operating losses ($4,935M in 2023 vs. $3,519M in 2022), showing inability to cover interest expenses with earnings.
- Pension/postretirement liabilities rose $495M to $8,077M (2023), while purchase obligations surged $61,963M to $141,122M, amplifying solvency risks.
- Negative equity and worsening operational performance (deepening losses) intensified concerns about long-term viability and capacity to meet financial obligations.
- Debt growth, escalating liabilities, and persistent operational losses collectively deteriorated solvency metrics, indicating heightened financial risk.

Efficiency Analysis

- DIO decreased from 452 to 415 days due to optimized production/supply chain efficiency, air traffic recovery, and resolved supply chain disruptions.
- DSO improved from 14 to 12 days, reflecting faster receivables collection from enhanced cash management and higher 2023 revenues.
- DPO increased from 59 to 62 days as Boeing negotiated longer supplier payment terms to conserve cash amid inflationary pressures.
- Inventory turnover accelerated despite marginal inventory value growth ($78.15B to $79.74B), indicating better demand alignment and operational execution.
- CCC gains were supported by commercial aviation market stabilization and strategic focus on working capital optimization per MD&A disclosures.
- Extended payables strategy offset ongoing supply chain challenges, balancing cash flow preservation with supplier relationships.
