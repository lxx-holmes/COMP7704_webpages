- Net Profit Margin increased from 7.2% (2020) to 12.7% (2021) due to operational efficiency, cost management, and absence of restructuring costs concluded in 2019.

- Gross Profit Margin remained stable (30.3% to 30.6%) with LIFO inventory method supporting consistent cost control despite revenue changes.
- Operating Profit Margin improved from 10.9% to 13.5% through manufacturing cost reductions and operational improvements.
- Foreign currency translation impacts in other income/expense contributed positively to profitability ratios.
- Derivative instruments stabilized profits by managing currency, interest rate, and commodity price risks.
- Restructuring costs from prior years (concluded in 2019) were absent in 2021, boosting profitability metrics.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current ratio decreased from 1.53 (2020) to 1.45 (2021) as current liabilities grew 16% ($25,717M to $29,847M) compared to 10% current asset growth ($39,464M to $43,455M).
- Quick ratio declined from 1.09 to 0.986 due to a $2.6B inventory increase ($11,402M to $14,038M) being excluded from this ratio calculation.
- Cash ratio dropped from 0.36 to 0.31 as cash reserves decreased slightly ($9,352M to $9,254M) while current liabilities expanded.
- Reduced liquidity ratios indicate tighter short-term financial flexibility, particularly regarding inventory-dependent obligations.
- Inventory growth suggests potential turnover challenges, requiring management attention to optimize working capital efficiency.
- Despite declines, ratios remain within stable operational ranges, though continued monitoring of liability management is advised.

- Equity increased through unrealized gains in securities ($1.2B AOCI growth) and retained earnings, offsetting a minor $0.3B total debt increase from new issuances.

- Structured refinancing activities maintained debt maturity profile while optimizing interest costs, contributing to improved solvency metrics.

Efficiency Analysis

- Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) decreased from 63.9 to 60.7 days due to enhanced receivables management and improved collection processes, boosting liquidity.
- Days Payable Outstanding (DPO) increased from 76.9 to 83.9 days, reflecting extended supplier payment terms that retained cash longer and improved cash flow.
- Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO) remained stable at 144.3 days (2021) vs. 143.3 days (2020), indicating consistent inventory turnover and management practices.
- The combined effect of reduced DSO and increased DPO demonstrates optimized cash flow management, while steady DIO shows no material operational inefficiencies.
- The CCC improvement reflects stronger cash conversion efficiency through better working capital strategies in receivables and payables.
