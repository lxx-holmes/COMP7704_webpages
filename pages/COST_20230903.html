- Operating profit margin decreased from 3.43% to 3.35% as merchandise costs rose ($199,382M to $212,586M) and SG&A expenses increased ($19,779M to $21,590M), outpacing revenue growth.
- Gross profit margin improved from 12.1% to 12.3% due to merchandise cost increases being offset by higher net sales growth.
- ROA remained stable at 9.12% (2023) vs. 9.11% (2022), indicating proportional growth in net income and total assets.
- ROE declined from 28.31% to 25.13% due to $452M in cash dividends declared in 2023, increasing equity without proportional net income growth.
- Stability across most ratios reflects effective financial management despite minor fluctuations from operating cost pressures and equity changes.

- Improved liquidity metrics indicate enhanced operational efficiency in managing obligations, inventory, and cash generation.
