Profitability Analysis

- Net profit margin improved from 2.596% (2023) to 2.89% (2024) due to enhanced cost management and operational efficiency, including logistics system optimizations.
- Gross profit margin increased slightly from 12.26% to 12.6%, reflecting better merchandise cost control through direct supplier purchases and depot routing strategies.
- Operating profit margin rose from 3.35% to 3.65%, driven by reduced handling/distribution costs and resource efficiency improvements.
- Return on Assets (ROA) increased from 9.13% to 10.55%, indicating improved asset utilization through higher sales per dollar of assets and inventory turnover optimization.
- Return on Equity (ROE) surged from 25.12% to 31.16%, attributed to $700 million in share repurchases and effective reinvestment of retained earnings.
- Asset turnover ratio improved from 3.513 to 3.646, reflecting efficient inventory management and revenue generation per asset unit.
- Total revenue growth across U.S., Canada, and international segments (Note 11) combined with margin expansion boosted overall profitability.
- High sales volumes and rapid inventory turnover from Costco's low-price, limited-selection business model supported profitability gains.
- All profitability ratios showed year-over-year improvement, signaling comprehensive operational efficiency and strategic financial management.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current ratio decreased from 1.068 (2023) to 0.966 (2024) as current liabilities growth ($33.6B to $35.5B) outpaced current assets ($35.9B to $34.2B), indicating minor liquidity decline.
- Quick ratio fell from 0.573 to 0.439 due to reduced cash reserves and increased liabilities, reflecting weaker liquidity without inventory reliance.
- Cash ratio declined from 0.453 to 0.314, driven by $13.7B to $9.9B cash reduction from $9.0B dividends, $700M stock buybacks, and $1.0B debt repayment.
- Current liabilities increased by $1.9B (2023-2024), primarily from higher accounts payable and other liabilities, straining liquidity despite inventory growth.
- Cash reserves dropped $3.8B year-over-year due to aggressive shareholder returns ($9.7B total) and $1.0B Senior Notes repayment in May 2024.
- Operating cash flow of $11.3B was offset by $10.7B financing outflows and capital expenditures, reducing liquid asset buffers.
- Liquidity ratios weakened across all metrics, signaling tighter short-term flexibility, though positions remain near manageable thresholds.
- Merchandise inventories grew but were excluded from quick/cash ratios, limiting their positive impact on overall liquidity assessment.
- Company should monitor cash management and liability growth to avoid strain from debt obligations and shareholder distribution commitments.

Solvency Analysis

- Debt-to-Equity ratio increased from 1.75 (2023: $43,936M liabilities / $25,058M equity) to 1.95 (2024: $46,209M liabilities / $23,622M equity), driven by $2,273M higher liabilities (including $500M from new Japanese Senior Notes) and $1,436M equity reduction from retained earnings usage.
- Interest Coverage ratio improved from 53.0 ($8,487M EBIT / $160M interest) to 57.6 ($9,740M EBIT / $169M interest) due to stronger EBIT growth (+14.8%) outpacing interest expense rise (+5.6%).
- Total liabilities rose partly from long-term debt increases, despite 2024 debt repayments, while equity declined due to operational use of retained earnings.

Efficiency Analysis

- DSO increased from 3.51 days (2023) to 3.97 days (2024), indicating slower receivables collections, likely influenced by changes in payment terms or customer behavior.
- DIO rose from 28.6 days (2023) to 30.6 days (2024), reflecting slower inventory turnover due to supply chain diversification efforts and expanded private-label manufacturing.
- DPO increased from 30.0 days (2023) to 31.9 days (2024), showing extended supplier payment timelines, attributed to negotiated terms that improved cash flow management.
- CCC improved slightly from 2.11 days (2023) to 2.68 days (2024), driven by the larger DPO increase offsetting DSO/DIO impacts, enhancing cash conversion efficiency despite inventory and receivables challenges.
- Strategies like supply chain resilience initiatives and supplier negotiations align with these metric shifts, though continued monitoring of receivables and inventory turnover is recommended.
