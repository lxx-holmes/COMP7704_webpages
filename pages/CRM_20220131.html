Profitability Analysis

- Net profit margin declined from 19.2% (2021) to 5.4% (2022) due to Slack acquisition integration costs ($0.7B advertising expense in 2020 rising to $1.0B in 2022) and higher operational expenses.
- Gross profit margin decreased slightly from 74.6% (2021) to 73.6% (2022), reflecting revenue recognition complexity from multi-obligation contracts and rising costs.
- Operating profit margin remained stable at 2.1% (2022 vs. 2021), indicating consistent expense management despite overall cost pressures.
- ROA dropped from 6.1% (2021) to 1.5% (2022), and ROE fell from 9.8% to 2.5%, driven by lower net income and $2.3B in 2022 capitalized contract costs (up from $1.1B in 2020) being amortized over time.
- Revenue growth was offset by disproportionate expense increases, including $2.3B in capitalized costs for obtaining revenue contracts and acquisition-related operational inefficiencies.
- Profitability challenges stem from upfront costs of large acquisitions, rising advertising expenses, and complex revenue allocation processes requiring improved cost controls.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current ratio declined from 1.23 (2021) to 1.05 (2022) as current liabilities grew from $17.7B to $21.8B, driven by accounts payable, accrued expenses, and a $2.6B increase in unearned revenue from subscription prepayments.
- Quick ratio dropped from 1.11 to 0.93, indicating reduced ability to cover obligations without inventory liquidation; cash ratio fell from 0.35 to 0.25 due to cash reserves decreasing from $6.2B to $5.5B, partially from Slack's $14.9B acquisition.
- Cash outflows for acquisitions (e.g., Slack) and reliance on less-liquid marketable securities contributed to lower cash ratios, though accounts receivable and securities remained stable.
- Unearned revenue growth ($2.6B in 2022) reflects subscription-model strength but increases current liabilities, temporarily pressuring liquidity despite future revenue assurance.
- Rising goodwill/intangible assets from acquisitions added long-term value but strained short-term liquidity via integration costs and liability increases.
- Current ratio staying above 1 confirms solvency, but management must balance liability growth and cash reserves to maintain financial flexibility.

- While solvency remains stable, management must prioritize debt level monitoring and profitability improvement to mitigate rising leverage risks.
