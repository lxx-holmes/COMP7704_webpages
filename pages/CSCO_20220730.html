Profitability Analysis

- Gross margin declined from 64.0% (2021) to 62.5% (2022) due to $1.5B increase in product cost of sales, unfavorable product mix, and productivity issues in the Americas segment.
- Operating profit margin improved from 25.7% to 27.1% driven by reduced sales/marketing expenses and restructuring charges dropping from $886M to $6M.
- Net profit margin increased from 21.2% to 22.9% as lower restructuring costs and expense controls offset gross margin pressures.
- ROA rose from 10.8% to 12.5% and ROE from 25.6% to 29.7%, reflecting improved net income and more efficient asset/equity utilization.
- Product revenue grew 6% while service revenue declined 2%, creating cost absorption challenges partially mitigated by operational efficiency gains.
- Profitability improvements occurred despite gross margin headwinds, primarily through disciplined cost management and resolution of prior restructuring programs.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current ratio decreased to 1.43 (2022) from 1.49 (2021), reflecting reduced short-term coverage capacity but remaining stable above 1, partially due to $1.0B inventory increase from Russia-Ukraine war/COVID-19 supply chain disruptions.
- Quick ratio declined to 1.33 (2022) from 1.43 (2021), impacted by lower liquid assets and higher inventory levels, though still above 1 indicating manageable liquidity.
- Cash ratio dropped to 0.75 (2022) from 0.93 (2021) due to $2.1B reduction in cash equivalents and $3.2B decline in investments, raising concerns about immediate liquidity without asset sales.
- Working capital fell to $11,077M (2022) from $12,855M (2021), driven by reduced free cash flow ($12.7B vs. $14.8B) and significant cash outflows including $7.7B stock repurchases and $6.2B dividends.
- Liquidity pressures intensified from $13.9B shareholder returns and acquisitions, but overall stability persists with current/quick ratios above 1, requiring closer cash management monitoring moving forward.

Solvency Analysis

- Debt-to-Equity Ratio remained stable at 1.36 in both 2021 ($56,222M liabilities / $41,275M equity) and 2022 ($54,229M liabilities / $39,773M equity), reflecting proportional declines in liabilities and equity.
- Interest Coverage Ratio improved from 29.6 (2021 EBIT $12,833M / interest $434M) to 38.8 (2022 EBIT $13,969M / interest $360M) due to higher earnings and lower interest costs.
- Solvency Ratio (Total Liabilities/Equity) held steady at 1.36 both years, indicating consistent long-term financial health and leverage management.
- Revenue growth ($51,557M in 2022 vs. $49,818M in 2021) and reduced restructuring charges ($6M vs. $886M) boosted operating income, supporting EBIT expansion.
- Effective financial management maintained ratio stability despite $2B liabilities reduction and $1.5B equity decline between 2021-2022.
- Lower interest expenses (-17% YoY) combined with higher EBIT (+9% YoY) enhanced interest coverage capacity.
- Stable leverage profile demonstrates controlled financial risk despite active balance sheet adjustments.

Efficiency Analysis

- DSO increased to 46.8 days in 2022 (vs. 42.2 in 2021) due to COVID-19-related customer payment delays and extended collection terms.
- DIO rose sharply to 48.5 days (vs. 31.8 in 2021) from supply chain disruptions causing inventory hoarding and logistics delays to mitigate component shortages.
- DPO decreased to 43.1 days (vs. 48.1 in 2021) as Cisco prioritized faster supplier payments to secure inventory amid supply constraints, straining cash reserves.
- Cash Conversion Cycle worsened by 25.3 days to 52.2 in 2022, reflecting combined inefficiencies from slower receivables, prolonged inventory turnover, and accelerated payables.
- Pandemic impacts reduced customer order predictability, increased collection risks, and created nonlinear sales patterns affecting inventory management.
- Supply chain challenges led to 41% inventory value increase (per financial notes) to prevent stockouts, despite higher holding costs.
- Recommendations include optimizing AR collections, improving inventory turnover through demand forecasting, and balancing supplier payment terms with cash flow needs.
- Financial statements cite COVID-19 as primary driver of extended lead times, 15%+ supply chain cost increases, and $2B inventory commitment growth.
