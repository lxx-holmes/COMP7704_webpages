- Return on Assets rose from -2.31% to 6.53%, reflecting higher asset utilization and operational efficiency as costs grew slower than revenues despite increased crude oil expenses.
- Return on Equity increased from -4.18% to 11.16%, driven by $785M after-tax asset sale gains ($755M upstream) and stable operational expenses amid revenue growth.
- Return on Capital Employed improved from -2.8% to 9.4%, supported by reduced Asset Retirement Obligations ($12.8B in 2021 vs. $13.6B in 2020) and better capital allocation.
- Return on Stockholders' Equity rose from -4.0% to 11.5%, aided by $5.95B tax expense reflecting higher pre-tax profits vs. a $1.89B tax benefit in 2020 during losses.

- Absence of 2020's large-scale upstream asset impairments allowed 2021 net income to benefit fully from improved oil/gas pricing and demand.
