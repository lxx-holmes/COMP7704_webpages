- Quick Ratio decreased from 0.94x to 0.83x as liquid assets (excluding inventories) grew insufficiently to offset $5B liability growth from higher accounts payable and deferred income.

- Current liabilities surged 9.6% year-over-year, driven primarily by progress collections and deferred income obligations.
- Marginal 0.17% reduction in current assets amplified liquidity pressure despite improved receivables and contract assets.
- Stable cash reserves demonstrated maintained emergency liquidity, but reliance on non-cash assets increased for routine obligations.
- Inventory levels remained consistent, indicating the Quick Ratio decline stemmed from liability growth rather than stockpiling.
- Working capital management requires enhanced focus as liability growth outpaces liquid asset accumulation.

- Equity Ratio declined slightly from 20.9% (2021) to 20.0% (2022), signaling greater debt financing of assets compared to equity.

- Rising leverage and reduced equity financing align with GE's strategic debt utilization for growth initiatives while achieving better interest expense coverage.
