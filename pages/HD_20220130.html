Profitability Analysis

- Gross profit margin declined slightly from 33.9% (2020) to 33.6% (2021) due to higher inventory costs, evidenced by merchandise inventories increasing from $16,627M to $22,068M.
- Operating profit margin improved from 13.8% to 15.2% through better expense management, supported by reduced cash outflows from operating activities.
- Net profit margin increased from 9.7% to 10.9% driven by higher net earnings ($12,866M in 2020 vs. $16,433M in 2021).
- ROA rose significantly from 18.2% to 22.9% as asset utilization improved, with total assets growing modestly ($70,581M to $71,876M) alongside substantial earnings growth.
- ROE turned negative in 2021 due to a stockholders' equity deficit (-$1,696M vs. +$3,299M in 2020), primarily from $14,809M spent on share repurchases.
- Operational efficiency gains were offset by equity structure concerns, requiring attention to debt levels and capital allocation strategies.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current ratio declined from 1.23x (2020) to 1.01x (2021) due to reduced cash reserves ($7,895M to $2,343M) and increased current liabilities ($23,166M to $28,693M), signaling weakened short-term debt coverage.
- Quick ratio dropped from 0.51x (2020) to 0.24x (2021) as inventory surged 32.8% ($16,627M to $22,068M), tying up capital in less liquid assets.
- Cash ratio fell sharply from 0.34x (2020) to 0.08x (2021) due to $14.8B share repurchases, $7.0B dividends, and $7.8B HD Supply acquisition depleting cash reserves.
- Working capital strain intensified with 15.6% accounts payable growth ($11,606M to $13,462M) and 14.8% receivables increase ($2,992M to $3,426M), though inventory buildup dominated liquidity pressures.
- Operating cash flow decreased $2.3B year-over-year due to inventory accumulation and higher transportation/product costs, despite improved vendor payment terms.
- Capital expenditures rose to $2.6B (2021 from $2.4B in 2020), compounding cash reserve pressures from strategic investments.
- COVID-19 disruptions caused inventory shrink estimation challenges, with 2020 estimates based on limited physical counts and 2021 resumption of full inventory audits.
- While current ratio remains above 1, reliance on inventory sales to meet obligations raises concerns given supply chain volatility and reduced cash buffers.

Solvency Analysis

- Debt-to-Equity ratio became non-meaningful in 2022 (negative equity) versus 20.4 in 2021, driven by $14.8B share repurchases and $7.0B dividends in 2021 that reduced equity from $3,299M to -$1,696M.
- Interest coverage ratio improved from 13.6x (2021) to 17.1x (2022) due to net earnings increasing from $12,866M to $16,433M.
- Solvency ratio rose above 1 (1.023x in 2022 vs 0.954x in 2021) as liabilities grew to $73,572M (+$6.29B) while assets only increased to $71,876M (+$1.295B), partly from new senior notes and commercial paper borrowings.
- Negative equity and liabilities exceeding assets in 2022 indicate potential financial stress despite improved earnings capacity for interest payments.
- Total debt increased through new issuances while shareholder equity erosion accelerated via capital return programs, creating mixed solvency signals.

Efficiency Analysis

- Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) remained stable at 8.27 days (2021) vs. 8.26 days (2020), indicating consistent accounts receivable management.
- Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO) increased significantly from 69.62 days (2020) to 80.16 days (2021), driven by COVID-19 supply chain disruptions, labor shortages, and demand forecasting challenges.
- Days Payable Outstanding (DPO) showed minimal change at 49.00 days (2021) vs. 48.55 days (2020), reflecting stable supplier payment practices.
- Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) rose from 29.33 days (2020) to 39.43 days (2021) due to prolonged inventory turnover, with operational inefficiencies from pandemic-related distribution delays and online order surges.
- Supply chain constraints caused inventory stockpiling and restocking delays, while labor shortages at fulfillment centers slowed inventory movement through the supply chain.
- Increased CCC signals reduced liquidity efficiency, as inventory-to-cash conversion slowed despite unchanged receivables collection and payables management.
