Profitability Analysis

- Gross margin ratio improved from 33.0% (2021) to 33.3% (2022) due to reduced production costs, freight expenses, and distribution network efficiencies.
- Operating profit margin increased from 10.77% to 12.56%, driven by lower selling, general, and administrative expenses and operational cost controls.
- Net profit margin rose from 6.51% to 8.77% as higher gross margins and disciplined expense management offset tax and interest obligations.
- ROA improved from 12.48% to 18.9% through better inventory management, reduced shrinkage, and optimized asset utilization.
- ROE plummeted from 406.3% to -175.3% due to negative equity caused by aggressive stock repurchases, deferred revenue adjustments, and potential debt-related losses.
- Negative equity in 2022 stemmed from $14.3 billion in common stock buybacks, deferred revenue recognition timing, and self-insurance liability adjustments based on actuarial assumptions.
- Deferred revenue (e.g., stored-value cards, protection plans) impacted equity by delaying recognition until performance obligations were met.
- Vendor fund accounting complexities risked misclassification of rebates/discounts, potentially affecting gross margin and inventory valuation accuracy.
- Self-insurance liabilities increased, with claim accruals tied to actuarial estimates, introducing volatility to expenses and equity.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current ratio decreased from 1.19x (2021) to 1.02x (2022) due to current liabilities rising from $18,730M to $19,668M and reduced current assets, indicating tighter short-term coverage.
- Quick ratio fell sharply from 0.33x to 0.12x as merchandise inventory increased from $16,193M to $17,605M while cash reserves declined, reducing liquid asset coverage.
- Cash ratio dropped from 0.25x to 0.06x with cash equivalents plunging from $4,690M to $1,133M, exacerbated by $13,074M stock repurchases and lower operating cash flows ($11,049M to $10,113M).
- Liquidity decline reflects increased inventory dependence (less liquid assets) and higher liabilities, signaling working capital inefficiencies and urgent need for improved cash management or financing.

- Store closing lease obligations contributed marginally to liability management, with slight reductions via terminations.
- Despite stable interest coverage, negative equity and rising debt levels indicate deteriorating long-term financial stability.

- Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO) rose from 98.4 to 100 days due to merchandise return reserves and commodity price volatility, with inventory growing from $16,193M to $17,605M.
- Days Payable Outstanding (DPO) decreased from 66.1 to 64.5 days as supplier financing programs expanded, reducing payment periods despite $3.0 billion in outstanding obligations (2022).
- Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) extended from 36.1 to 39.5 days, driven by higher DIO/DSO partially offset by lower DPO; overall working capital efficiency remained strong.

- Accounts payable grew from $10,884M to $11,354M, reflecting strategic use of supplier financing to manage cash flow.
