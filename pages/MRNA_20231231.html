Profitability Analysis

- Net profit margin fell from 43.4% (2022) to -68.9% (2023) due to COVID-19 vaccine market transitioning to seasonal demand, lower sales volume, higher costs, and inventory write-offs.
- Operating profit margin dropped from 48.9% to -61.9% as operational inefficiencies and increased R&D/manufacturing expenses strained profitability.
- ROA declined from 32.3% to -25.6% and ROE from 43.7% to -34.0%, reflecting poor asset utilization and reduced returns exacerbated by shifting market conditions.
- Gross profit margin decreased sharply from 71.9% to 31.5%, driven by higher cost of sales and reduced pricing power amid lower vaccine demand.
- R&D expenses surged to 70.7% of revenue (vs. 17.1% in 2022) from heavy investment in RSV/CMV clinical trials and manufacturing capabilities, prioritizing long-term growth over short-term profitability.
- Strategic manufacturing adjustments, including capacity scaling and inventory write-offs, incurred near-term costs but aimed to align operations with reduced seasonal demand.
- Operational challenges included inefficient resource allocation and higher expenses during market transition, worsening profitability metrics despite cost optimization efforts.

- Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO) decreased from 63.9 to 15.7 days, aided by $2.2B inventory write-downs (obsolete materials/excess stock) and inventory reduction from $949M to $202M through manufacturing resizing.
- Days Payable Outstanding (DPO) extended from 32.8 to 40.4 days, preserving cash by delaying supplier payments.
- Operating cash flow shifted from $4.98B inflow (2022) to $3.1B outflow (2023), impacted by lower net income and working capital adjustments despite CCC efficiency gains.
- Inventory write-downs of $2.2B in 2023 resulted from reduced COVID-19 vaccine demand, leading to strategic inventory management and manufacturing scale-downs.
- Improved CCC efficiency offset higher DSO through combined DIO reduction and DPO extension, enhancing overall cash flow management despite operating cash outflow.
