Profitability Analysis

- Net profit margin declined from 17.2% (2021) to 14.2% (2022) due to 10.6% increase in cost of revenues ($19.2B) and $1.773B foreign currency revenue reduction, despite 6.3% revenue growth.
- Operating profit margin fell from 20.8% to 17.8% as marketing expenses rose 2.3% ($2.5B) and technology/development costs surged 16.6% ($2.7B), with operating income dropping 9.2%.
- ROA decreased from 11.5% to 9.2% due to 12.4% net income decline ($5.1B to $4.5B) and marginally lower asset turnover (66.6% to 65.1%).
- ROE plummeted from 32.3% to 21.6% driven by reduced net income and potential equity base expansion, signaling diminished shareholder returns.
- Gross profit margin contracted from 41.6% to 39.4% as content production/licensing costs outpaced 6.3% revenue growth, with cost of revenues increasing faster than sales.
- Asset turnover slightly declined (66.6% to 65.1%) reflecting less efficient asset utilization amid rising content/operational costs.
- Content costs surged due to upfront cash payments for production, increased amortization expenses (+$1.3B vs 2021), and growing content liabilities (+$1.5B).
- Foreign exchange headwinds impacted 56% of international revenues, weakening non-USD currencies against the dollar.
- Operational inefficiencies emerged from combined 2.3% marketing and 16.6% technology spending increases alongside 9.2% operating income decline.
- Profitability pressures require improved cost controls, operational efficiency measures, and currency risk mitigation strategies.
