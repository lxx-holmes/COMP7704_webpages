Profitability Analysis

- Gross margin ratio increased from 56.9% (2023) to 72.7% (2024) due to 217% Data Center revenue growth and reduced inventory provisions (7.5% to 2.7% impact), reflecting improved cost management and pricing strategies.
- Operating margin surged from 15.6% to 54.1%, driven by high-margin Data Center segment growth and revenue scaling outpacing R&D/SG&A expense increases.
- Net profit margin rose from 16.2% to 48.8%, supported by higher gross/operating margins, a one-time tax benefit, and increased interest income.
- ROA improved from 10.6% to 45.3%, indicating enhanced asset utilization through tripled Data Center revenue and operational efficiency.
- ROE increased from 19.7% to 69.2%, reflecting efficient equity use and net income growth despite higher shareholder equity.
- ROIC rose from 12.9% to 60.3% due to successful investments in high-return projects like Hopper GPUs and optimized capital deployment.
- Data Center revenue concentration (few large customers) enhanced pricing power and volume benefits, while Gaming revenue grew moderately.
- Effective tax rate of 12% in 2024 (vs. prior-year tax benefit) supported net income growth despite transitioning to a tax expense.
- Operating expenses grew slower than revenue, with R&D/SG&A increases offset by margin expansion from scaled operations.

Liquidity Analysis

- Quick Ratio rose from 2.73 to 3.67, driven by increased liquid assets excluding inventories, signaling improved working capital management and short-term obligation coverage.
- Cash Ratio increased from 0.52 to 0.68, bolstered by higher cash reserves relative to liabilities, enhancing immediate payment capacity.
- Operational efficiency gains from robust operating cash flow and optimized working capital management contributed to liquidity improvements.
- Current liabilities grew moderately but were offset by faster asset expansion, resulting in enhanced financial flexibility and short-term stability.

- Debt-to-Equity ratio decreased from 0.86 (2023) to 0.53 (2024) due to repayment of 2023 notes and equity growth via retained earnings or stock issuance, lowering financial risk.
- Interest Coverage ratio surged from 16.0 to 131.6, driven by reduced interest obligations after retiring 2023 notes, significantly reducing default risk.
- Financial Leverage ratio declined from 1.86 to 1.53, reflecting reduced debt financing of assets through potential asset sales or equity increases from retained earnings.
- Long-term Debt to Capitalization ratio dropped from 30% to 16%, attributed to 2023 note repayments and equity expansion, signaling reduced long-term debt reliance.
- Strategic debt reduction and equity growth across all solvency ratios strengthened financial stability, lowered risk, and enhanced long-term viability.

- DIO decreased from 162.1 to 115.9 days, signaling faster inventory turnover due to reduced inventory provisions and strong demand-driven sales.
- DPO rose from 37.5 to 59.3 days, indicating improved cash flow through extended supplier payment terms, supported by better negotiation strategies.
- Lower CCC enhances working capital efficiency, reduces inventory holding costs, and boosts liquidity by accelerating resource-to-cash conversion.
- Slight DSO increase deemed manageable given revenue expansion, while DIO and DPO improvements reflect operational optimization in inventory and payables.
