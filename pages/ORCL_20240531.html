Profitability Analysis

- Net profit margin increased from 17.03% (2023) to 19.77% (2024), driven by cloud/license segment revenue growth and higher-margin cloud services improving profitability.
- Operating profit margin rose from 26.21% to 28.97%, reflecting enhanced operational efficiency from strategic adjustments and cloud business expansion.
- Return on Assets (ROA) improved from 6.33% to 7.42% due to better asset utilization in cloud infrastructure, particularly data centers, despite no segment-specific asset tracking.
- Return on Equity (ROE) dropped from 546.4% to 113.3% primarily from increased equity levels in 2024, though remains strong; 2023's extreme ROE resulted from unusually low equity.
- Cloud/license segment outperformed with revenue growth outpacing expenses, boosting margins through scalable cloud solutions and license renewals.
- Hardware segment saw slight revenue decline but maintained stable margins via cost management and operational discipline.
- Services segment experienced revenue dip and margin contraction despite controlled expenses, indicating weaker demand or competitive pressures.
- Overall profitability gains reflect Oracle's strategic shift toward cloud-based solutions, offsetting challenges in hardware/services segments.
- Higher 2024 equity levels normalized ROE while improved asset efficiency (ROA) and margins signal stronger operational execution across core businesses.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current ratio declined from 0.909 (2023) to 0.715 (2024), and cash ratio fell from 0.423 to 0.331, driven by current liabilities increasing $8,454M (outpacing asset growth).
- Current liabilities surged 37% ($23,090M to $31,544M) primarily from higher notes payable/borrowings.
- Current assets grew $1,550M ($21,004M to $22,554M), insufficient to counter liability expansion.
- Cash/cash equivalents increased modestly ($9,765M to $10,454M), but $3.5B senior note repayments and capital expenditures reduced cash coverage capacity.
- Liquidity deterioration reflects weakened short-term financial flexibility, necessitating closer monitoring of working capital management and debt levels.

Solvency Analysis

- Debt-to-Equity ratio improved from 85.33x (2023) to 14.26x (2024) due to $3.5B debt repayment and equity surge from $1,556M to $9,239M via retained earnings/issuance.
- Interest Coverage ratio increased from 3.73x (2023) to 4.37x (2024) as EBIT grew 17% to $15,353M, strengthening interest payment capacity.
- Total liabilities decreased from $132,828M (2023) to $131,737M (2024) through strategic debt management, including terminating/renegotiating term loans.
- Operating cash flow rose 9% in 2024, enabling debt reduction and supporting equity growth without excessive borrowing.
- Improved leverage reflects reduced long-term risk, with equity now representing 6.5% of total capital (up from 1.2% in 2023).
- Debt restructuring efforts optimized Oracle's capital structure, replacing high-interest obligations with more favorable terms.
