Profitability Analysis

- Net Profit Margin declined from 61.6% (2021) to 56.2% (2022) due to increased expenses from Duke Realty's $23.2B acquisition and integration costs.
- Return on Assets (ROA) dropped from 5.0% to 3.8%, reflecting reduced efficiency as newly acquired 144M sq ft portfolio required time to optimize profitability.
- Return on Equity (ROE) decreased from 7.7% to 5.8% due to equity dilution from acquisition financing and lower net income.
- Operating Profit Margin fell sharply from 67.4% to 58.0% from higher operating expenses, including maintenance and leasing costs for newly integrated properties.
- Duke Realty acquisition expanded assets but included non-strategic properties marked for sale, temporarily lowering asset productivity.
- Equity dilution occurred as Prologis issued new shares to fund the acquisition, increasing the equity base without immediate profit growth.
- Auditors flagged critical challenges in assessing property holding periods, with potential impairments affecting profitability if holding periods shortened.
- Integration complexities and operational scaling efforts contributed to reduced cost control across all profitability metrics.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current ratio declined from 0.186 (2021) to 0.169 (2022), with quick ratios mirroring this trend, indicating insufficient liquid assets to cover short-term liabilities.
- Cash ratio dropped from 0.027 (2021) to 0.009 (2022) due to cash reserves halving from $556,117K to $278,483K, severely limiting immediate liquidity.
- Total debt surged by $6.16B (to $23.88B in 2022) primarily from the $23.2B Duke Realty acquisition, increasing liabilities and pressuring liquidity ratios.
- As a REIT, Prologis holds illiquid long-term real estate assets, relying on operational cash flow ($2.5B+ operating income) rather than liquid reserves to meet obligations.
- Year-over-year liquidity decline reflects strategic debt-fueled expansion but is partially offset by strong operational cash generation typical of REIT business models.
- Low ratios (current, quick, cash) highlight reliance on consistent cash flow from operations to service short-term debts, necessitating close monitoring of financial flexibility.

- Operational performance improved through higher rental income from acquired Duke Realty assets, supporting earnings despite rising interest costs.
- The company maintained balanced growth strategy with conservative debt use and robust capacity to service obligations.
