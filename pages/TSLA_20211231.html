Profitability Analysis

- Net profit margin increased from 2.29% (2020) to 10.25% (2021) due to higher revenues and cost management, with R&D expenses maintained at 5% of revenue and SG&A expenses decreasing from 10% to 8% of revenue.
- Gross profit margin rose from 21.04% to 25.28%, driven by 65% growth in automotive sales revenue from 433,815 additional Model 3/Y deliveries and cost efficiencies from localized production in China (Gigafactory Shanghai).
- Operating profit margin doubled from 6.32% to 12.12%, supported by automotive gross margin improvements (25.6% to 29.3%) and higher-margin services/retail merchandise sales.
- Return on Assets (ROA) surged from 1.38% to 8.89% through $5.55B increased operating cash flow and optimized asset utilization at Gigafactories.
- Return on Equity (ROE) jumped from 3.24% to 18.28% due to net income growth and reduced leverage (debt-to-equity ratio improved from 1.28x to 1.01x via $5.20B debt repayments).
- Debt-to-equity ratio decline reflects a shift toward conservative capital structure, with no equity offerings in 2021 and focus on operational cash flow.
- Operational scalability in China and cost control measures contributed to $12.72B automotive cost reductions despite 48% growth in energy storage costs.
- SG&A expenses grew 44% YoY but became proportionally smaller (8% of revenue vs. 10%), demonstrating improved operational leverage.

Liquidity Analysis

- Current ratio declined from 1.88 (2020) to 1.37 (2021) due to $5.5B current liabilities increase outpacing $0.4B current assets growth, but remains above 1 indicating short-term obligation coverage ability.
- Quick ratio dropped from 1.49 to 0.995 as accounts payable/accrued liabilities surged from $2,102M to $4,578M and deferred revenue increased, reducing liquid asset coverage.
- Cash ratio fell from 1.36 to 0.89 due to $1.8B cash reduction from investments in property/equipment ($6,069M) and digital assets/marketable securities purchases.
- Operating cash flow of $11,497M was offset by $7,868M investing outflows and financing activity net outflows, straining reserves despite strong operational performance.
- COVID-19 disrupted supply chains, potentially slowing asset conversion to cash and increasing operational costs, exacerbating liquidity pressures.
- All liquidity ratios declined but remain within acceptable ranges, requiring ongoing monitoring of liability management and cash preservation strategies.

Solvency Analysis

- Debt-to-Equity ratio improved from 1.28 (2020) to 1.01 (2021) due to $422M and $474M convertible note settlements via 5.3M/6.5M stock issuances, reducing liabilities while increasing equity.
- Interest Coverage Ratio surged from 2.67 to 17.58, driven by EBIT growth from $1,994M (2020) to $6,523M (2021), reflecting stronger profitability and debt service capacity.
- Adoption of ASU 2020-06 accounting standards eliminated remaining debt discounts on convertible notes, reducing interest expense recognition and improving profitability metrics.
- Capital structure shifted toward non-recourse debt (secured by subsidiary assets) from recourse debt, lowering financial risk and enhancing long-term stability.
- Issued new lower-cost debt (e.g., 2024 Notes at 2.00% interest rate), reducing overall borrowing expenses and refinancing risks.
- Strategic debt restructuring and equity expansion improved investor/creditor appeal by demonstrating balanced leverage and effective risk management.

Efficiency Analysis

- Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) improved from -6.7 days (2020) to -25.8 days (2021), driven by enhanced working capital efficiency through faster collections, inventory turnover, and extended supplier payment terms.
- Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) decreased from 21.8 to 12.9 days, reflecting faster customer collections via improved accounts receivable practices (e.g., payment term adjustments or incentives).
- Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO) declined from 60.0 to 52.2 days due to accelerated inventory turnover, including a $106 million inventory write-down to clear excess stock.
- Days Payable Outstanding (DPO) increased from 88.5 to 90.9 days, indicating extended supplier payment terms to retain cash longer and improve liquidity.
- Combined DSO reduction (-8.9 days), DIO improvement (-7.8 days), and DPO expansion (+2.4 days) collectively strengthened cash flow efficiency and working capital management.
- Negative CCC progression highlights Tesla's ability to generate cash rapidly, supported by operational improvements in receivables, inventory, and payables management.
