Profitability Analysis

- Gross Profit Margin decreased from 25.6% (2022) to 18.25% (2023) due to higher production costs from factory expansions and competitive pricing pressures.
- Operating Profit Margin fell from 16.76% to 9.19% as R&D expenses rose 29% (Cybertruck, AI) and SG&A increased 22% (labor, infrastructure).
- Net Profit Margin remained stable at ~15.5% through effective tax management and non-operating cost controls despite gross/operating margin declines.
- ROA declined from 15.26% to 14.07% as asset growth (factories, machinery) outpaced net income growth, reflecting capital expenditure impacts.
- ROE dropped from 28.07% to 23.95% due to equity growth exceeding net income expansion.
- Operating cash flow decreased $1.47B due to lower net income, partially offset by working capital adjustments.
- Investing cash outflows rose to $8.90B (2023) from $7.16B (2022) for factory expansions and product development.
- Financing cash inflows increased via $3.93B debt issuances and reduced debt repayments ($2.01B less vs. 2022).
- Energy/storage segment gross margin improved from 7.4% to 18.9% driven by Megapack margin gains and IRA tax credits.
- Foreign currency exposure risks persist due to unhedged global operations.
- R&D expenses grew 29% in 2023 for Cybertruck pre-production and AI/autonomous driving development.
- SG&A costs rose 22% from workforce expansion and facility-related expenses.

- Equity growth was driven by stock issuances for compensation and retained earnings, while debt management included balancing new debt issuances with facility terminations.
- Operational cost pressures (including $3.97B R&D and rising SG&A) reduced EBIT, impacting the Interest Coverage Ratio despite strong overall solvency.
- Tesla maintains robust debt management with a 33% equity increase and manageable debt levels, ensuring stable interest coverage capacity.
