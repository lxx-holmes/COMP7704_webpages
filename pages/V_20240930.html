Profitability Analysis

- Net profit margin increased from 52.9% (2023) to 54.9% (2024), driven by 10% net revenue growth outpacing a 12% rise in client incentives, reflecting improved cost management and pricing strategies.
- Operating profit margin improved from 64.3% to 65.7%, supported by 11% data processing revenue growth and 9% service revenue growth, with operational cost control offsetting expense increases.
- Return on Assets (ROA) rose from 19.1% to 20.9%, attributed to technology investments and 10% processed transaction growth enhancing asset efficiency without proportional asset base expansion.
- Return on Equity (ROE) surged from 44.6% to 50.4%, reflecting efficient equity use through revenue/net income growth without significant additional equity investment, benefiting shareholders.
- Revenue growth (10% net revenue increase) stemmed from higher payments volume, international transactions, and processed transactions, outpacing client incentive costs (up 12%).
- Cost management contributed as operating expenses grew slower than revenue, including controlled network/processing costs alongside revenue expansion.
- Asset utilization improvements resulted from technology/equipment investments, enabling higher transaction processing efficiency and profit generation.
- Profitability gains position the company for continued growth through balanced revenue expansion, expense discipline, and operational leverage.

- Restricted cash equivalents for U.S. litigation escrow increased but remain unavailable for general operations, exacerbating liquidity constraints.
